Embedded Protocol Standards Side-by-Side Comparison

Feature Spec I2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit) SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface) UART (Universal Asynchronous)
Bus Pins Required 2 pins (SDA, SCL) 4 pins (MOSI, MISO, SCLK, CS) 2 pins (TX, RX)
Transmission Mode Half-Duplex (shared SDA line) Full-Duplex (separate MOSI/MISO) Full-Duplex (separate TX/RX lines)
Synchronicity Synchronous (Common SCL Clock) Synchronous (Common SCLK Clock) Asynchronous (No clock, relies on Baud rate)
Addressing Mechanism 7-bit / 10-bit software addressing in frame Physical hardware Slave-Select line (CS) None (point-to-point connection)
Overhead Efficiency Lower (~75% due to addr/ACK bits) Maximum (~98% due to zero protocol headers) Medium (~80% due to start/parity/stop bits)
Typical Speed Range 100kbps, 400kbps, up to 3.4Mbps 1MHz, 10MHz, exceeding 50MHz 9600bps up to 115200bps typically
Key Features & Limits Multi-master, clock stretching. Bus capacitance limit. Highest speeds. Pins consumption grows per slave. Simple. Timing critical. Mismatched bauds fail frames.
